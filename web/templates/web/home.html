Frequently Asked Questions

Let's answer some of the most popular travel questions.

Tanzania is generally safe for travelers, especially in popular safari and tourist regions. Like anywhere, it's wise to remain aware of your surroundings and follow travel advisories.

Commonly recommended vaccines include Yellow Fever, Typhoid, Hepatitis A/B, and routine vaccines. Check with your doctor for updated advice.

Stick to bottled or filtered water. Most safari lodges and hotels offer safe meals prepared with hygiene standards in mind.

Safari operators are trained to handle emergencies. Medical evacuation and travel insurance are strongly recommended.

Most visitors can obtain a visa on arrival or apply online for an eVisa. Requirements vary by nationality.
